The Sun

The Sun, our source of light, warmth, and life. Without the Sun, plants couldn't photosynthesize, oxygen couldn't be produced. Water wouldn't evaporate and turn into rain that waters our crops. Without the Sun, life on this planet would not be possible. Our warm ball of light keeps us alive and gives us a nice place to call home. What a great Star we have to call ours.

Sun Facts:

  • The Sun is approximately 4.603 Billion Years Old, the oldest object in our solar system
  • Our Sun is a yellow dwarf, meaning that it burns between 5027° and 5727 °C
  • The latin name for our Sun is Sol(Sun) or Solis(The Sun)
  • The Sun holds 99.86% of the mass of the solar system consisting of mostly hydrogen and helium
  • The Sun will eventually expand to a red giant, engulfing most of the inner planets
  • At the end of its life, our star will turn into a White Dwarf

Solar Flares

Solar Eclipses:

Solar Flares happen when large amounts of electromagnetic radiation are ejected into space from the surface of the Sun. Solar flares usually take place on active regions of the Sun, where strong magnetic fields are present typically noticable due to the dark sunspots on the star. When enough EM radiation builds up it gets released in a massive, dense solar flare. Solar flares can last from minutes to hours depending on their intensity.

Solar flares and Humans:

Thankfully, the atmosphere and Earth's magnetic field protect us from any radiation and danger that these flares may pose, but geomagnetic storms may occur as a result of the magnetic field intercepting the flare. A storm like this won't have any real impact on us on the surface but it may interfere with our radio communications and GPS signals worldwide. A positive consequence of a solar flare hitting the Earth is the beautiful Northern lights (Aurora Borealis) that happens as a result of stray particles interacting with our atmospheric gases to create that gorgeous glow.
